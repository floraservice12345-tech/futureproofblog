What is Claude 4 Sonnet?

Claude 4 Sonnet is the mid-tier model in Anthropic's Claude 4 family, positioned between Claude 4 Haiku (fast and economical) and Claude 4 Opus (maximum power). The "Sonnet" nomenclature reflects Anthropic's philosophy: Sonnet hits the best balance between intelligence and speed, making it the model most users will want for everyday professional tasks.

Claude 4 Sonnet features a 200,000-token context window, excellent code generation, and what many users consider the most natural, human-like writing of any AI model. Anthropic trains Claude with a focus on safety and honesty — it is less likely to generate harmful content and more likely to acknowledge uncertainty than competitors.

What Makes Claude 4 Sonnet Special

1. Best-in-Class Long-Form Writing

If you need an AI to write content that feels genuinely human — nuanced, varied in sentence structure, with a natural narrative flow — Claude 4 Sonnet is the best choice in 2026. Its writing avoids the repetitive patterns and stilted phrasing that often betray AI-generated content. For Indian content creators, bloggers, authors, and copywriters, this is Claude's killer feature.

Claude also excels at maintaining a consistent tone and voice across long documents. If you provide examples of your writing style, it adapts and maintains that style far better than GPT-5 or Gemini.

2. Exceptional 200K Context Window

Claude 4 Sonnet's 200,000-token context window allows it to process approximately 150,000 words in a single conversation. This is sufficient for most legal agreements, entire academic research papers, complete codebases of moderate size, and multi-chapter document reviews. While smaller than Gemini 2.5 Ultra's 1-million-token window, Claude maintains noticeably better coherence at the high end of its context range than some competitors.

3. Safety and Honesty

Anthropic's Constitutional AI training approach means Claude is significantly more likely to: admit when it doesn't know something, decline to generate harmful content even under pressure, flag potential errors in its own reasoning, and avoid confident hallucinations. For Indian professionals using AI for compliance, legal, or medical research, this honesty is a genuine practical advantage.

4. Code Generation

Claude 4 Sonnet is an excellent coding assistant — generally considered on par with or slightly below GPT-5 for pure code generation, but often preferred by developers for its cleaner code structure, better documentation generation, and superior ability to explain complex code in plain English. For Indian developers who need to work with legacy codebases or explain technical concepts to non-technical stakeholders, Claude's explanatory ability is particularly valuable.

5. Research and Analysis

Claude excels at synthesising information, identifying patterns across large amounts of text, and generating structured analysis reports. For Indian researchers, consultants, and analysts, Claude is particularly well-suited to tasks like literature reviews, competitive analysis, and summarising research findings into executive summaries.

Claude 4 Sonnet on Indian Tasks: Practical Use Cases

Use case: Writing a 2,000-word Article on Indian Personal Finance

Given a detailed brief to write an article about SIP investing for first-time investors in India — including fund categories, SEBI regulations, and tax implications under the new tax regime — Claude can produce a comprehensive, well-structured draft with relevant regulatory references and appropriate disclaimers. Its long-form writing is widely regarded as among the most natural of any AI model. As always, verify specific regulatory and tax details before publishing.

Use case: Reviewing a 50-Page Commercial Lease Agreement

Claude's large context window makes it well suited to reviewing a long document such as a 50-page commercial lease agreement — for example, summarising key obligations, flagging unusual clauses, and outlining potential risks for a tenant. It typically adds a caveat that a practising lawyer should review the full agreement, which is appropriate. Any legal document should always be reviewed by a qualified lawyer before you rely on it.

Use case: Generating Python Code for Data Analysis

Claude is also capable at coding tasks — for example, writing Python to process a CSV of Indian stock-market data, calculate rolling averages, and generate a Matplotlib chart formatted for Indian conventions (figures in lakhs/crores, Indian date format). In practice such code often runs with only minor adjustments, though you should always test generated code before using it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use Claude 4 Sonnet for free in India?

Yes, Claude.ai has a free tier but with limited access — it uses Claude 3.5 Haiku (the fastest, smallest model) and has daily usage limits. For full Claude 4 Sonnet access, the Claude Pro subscription at approximately ₹1,700/month is required. Note that UPI payment is not currently supported — you need an international credit/debit card.

Is Claude safe to use for sensitive business documents?

Claude has strong privacy policies and does not train on your conversations if you opt out (which is the default for paid plans). However, as with all cloud-based AI, you should avoid sharing highly sensitive information like passwords, financial account numbers, or personally identifiable information. For highly confidential work, consider running Claude via the API with enterprise privacy agreements.

Does Claude support Hindi?

Claude has some Hindi capability but it is limited compared to GPT-5 and Gemini 2.5 Ultra. It can understand Hindi input and generate Hindi output but makes more errors than its competitors in complex or formal Hindi. For Hindi-heavy workflows, Gemini 2.5 Ultra is the better choice.

What is the difference between Claude 4 Sonnet and Claude 4 Opus?

Claude 4 Opus is Anthropic's most powerful model — better reasoning, deeper analysis, higher quality outputs. However, it is slower and more expensive. For most everyday professional tasks, Claude 4 Sonnet offers 90% of Opus's quality at a fraction of the cost and with faster response times. Use Sonnet for daily work; upgrade to Opus for the most demanding analytical or creative tasks.
